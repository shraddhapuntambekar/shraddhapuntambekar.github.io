## Pharma R&D Lab Digital Maturity Assessment
**Client:** German pharmaceutical multinational · **Employer:** Cognizant · **Role:** Lab Informatics Scientific SME (Senior Associate)

### Problem
A global pharmaceutical company needed an objective assessment of how digitally mature their R&D laboratories were across multiple sites — to understand the current state, benchmark against industry peers, and prioritize digitalization investments.

### Approach
- Defined and owned the **maturity assessment framework** (strategic areas, levels, baseline criteria) and designed mapped survey and interview questions to enable accurate stakeholder engagement and heatmap scoring.
- Established assessment areas for **pharma benchmarking**, adopted by the team for comparative analysis; created structured forms and ran internal interviews to assess maturity across labs.
- **Stakeholder engagement:** customized interview questions per stakeholder lab; co-conducted **16+ interviews**; documented transcripts and meeting minutes for leadership alignment.
- Led interviews with internal **instrument-connectivity experts** to capture connectivity requirements and data types.
- Translated leadership requirements into actionable steps for the team; contributed to weekly leadership presentations; owned and developed the organizational chart.

### Outcome
- A scoring **heatmap and benchmarking framework** the client used to prioritize digital investments.
- Post-submission contributions to **dashboard and instrument-connectivity proposals**.

### Technologies & methods
Digital maturity assessment · Pharma benchmarking · Stakeholder interviews · Structured surveys · Heatmap scoring · Instrument connectivity · Strategic leadership artifacts

## Bioinformatics Pipeline Validation & Documentation
**Client:** British vaccine major · **Employer:** Cognizant · **Role:** Bioinformatics Expert / SME

### Problem
A British vaccine major was building a cloud-native bioinformatics analysis platform. They had **17 omics Nextflow pipelines** (genomics and transcriptomics) needing independent validation, and proteomics/metabolomics pipelines being engineered. They needed centralized error triage, a comprehensive developer documentation suite covering the full pipeline lifecycle, and architecture documentation capturing the as-is (on-prem) and to-be (on-cloud) states.

### Approach
- **Tested and validated 17 omics Nextflow pipelines** (genomics, transcriptomics) by executing them through Google Cloud Workflows, Seqera/Nextflow Tower, and locally.
- **Triaged observed errors** with engineering teams; documented root causes; maintained a troubleshooting knowledgebase.
- **Authored comprehensive developer documentation** covering:
  - Nextflow pipeline development — coding standards, modular design, testing
  - Pipeline publishing via Azure DevOps
  - Pipeline publishing via GitHub Actions
  - Pipeline execution via Google Cloud Workflows and Seqera/Nextflow Tower
  - FAQs and runbooks for common operational scenarios
- **Captured and documented** as-is (on-prem) and to-be (on-cloud) architectures of proteomics and metabolomics analysis pipelines, gathering details from engineering teams.

### Outcome
- 17 omics pipelines validated and triaged, with a documented error knowledgebase used by engineering teams.
- A developer documentation suite covering the full pipeline lifecycle (build → publish → execute).
- As-is / to-be architecture artifacts for proteomics and metabolomics workflows, used as the basis for cloud-migration planning.

### Technologies
Nextflow · nf-core · Google Cloud Workflows · Seqera/Nextflow Tower · Azure DevOps · GitHub Actions · Genomics & transcriptomics pipelines · Proteomics & metabolomics analysis

## Cancer Biomarker & Target Discovery
**Client / Employer:** NonExomics LLP · **Role:** Head of Computational Biology (2021–2023)

### Problem
NonExomics, an early-stage biotech startup, needed to identify novel cancer biomarkers and therapeutic targets at startup pace from proprietary and publicly available WGS and RNA-seq data — while building the bioinformatics function from scratch and managing a fully remote 3-person team across time zones.

### Approach
- Implemented and maintained **nf-core RNA-seq and WGS Nextflow pipelines on AWS**.
- Processed and analyzed proprietary and publicly available DNA and RNA expression datasets to identify novel cancer target candidates.
- **Designed siRNAs and qPCR primers** for bioinformatically predicted targets.
- Managed a fully remote 3-person team across time zones in a startup environment; defined delivery cadence and quality standards.
- Represented NonExomics at the startup meetup organized by VCR Park, Vizag, India.

### Outcome
- Candidate cancer biomarkers and therapeutic targets identified and **experimentally validated** by the wet-lab team.
- On-time delivery across geographies; established the company's bioinformatics function and team.

### Technologies
Nextflow · nf-core · AWS (EC2, S3) · WGS · RNA-seq · siRNA & qPCR primer design · Remote team management
